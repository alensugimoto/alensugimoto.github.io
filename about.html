ALEN SUGIMOTO

Hi, I'm Alen Sugimoto.

Detail-oriented undergraduate student with programming experience.
Competent in problem-solving and collaboration.
Eager to take on any challenge and grow in the field of computer science.

When I was a little boy, I would handcraft toys. I would assemble Lego Star Wars ships such as the X-Wing, construct airplanes and hobby kits, and make objects from scratch out of cardboard and tape. My imagination would bring the new toys to life. As I got older, I would build things that moved the way I could have only imagined before. I once made a putt-putt boat that pushed itself on water by the force resulting from the expulsion of steam from its simple engine. At school, I was able to design and build the winning sumo bot for a semester-end project in an Electronics 11 class. I also assembled a trebuchet that flung objects as far as four meters. At home, I enjoyed building balsa wood airplanes, which were able to fly with their propellers thrusting them forward. My passion for crafting these types of toys was the leading factor that connected me with computer science.

In grade ten, I enrolled in an in-class course on Visual Basic. Since programming relates to the kind of crafting I am passionate about, in which function takes priority over appearance, it was just as entertaining as making toys. Therefore, I eagerly taught myself the basics of Python and enrolled in another in-class course on Java.

In my senior year of high school, I completed a graduation project on a computer-science-related topic. Using MATLAB, I wrote an algorithm that finds the general shape of the fastest base running path from home to second base on a baseball field.

Due to all the experience, I am now following my passion for both crafting and programming through the field of computer science.
